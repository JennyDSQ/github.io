I am a Ph.D. candidate in economics at the University of Maryland. I am a macroeconomist studying topics in labor, consumption, and information friction. My current work studies how households and firms respond differently to aggregate shocks due to micro-level frictions and heterogeneous elasticities, as well as their implications for wages, consumption, inequality, and labor market dynamics.
